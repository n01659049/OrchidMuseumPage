Angraecum Magdalenae

What Makes This Orchid Special?

Appearance: This orchid features thick, olive green leaves with a light texture. The leaves begin folded at the base and then open flat. A mature plant can reach 12 inches in height.

Growth: Angraecum magdalenae produces multiple basal keikis (growths). These keikis flower after the main plant, extending the flowering period over several months.

Flowers: The orchid produces two to three large, round, crystalline white flowers. The flowers have a waxy, heavy substance to them and are described as having "an enticing nocturnally spicy fragrance".

Flowering Time: Flowers appear from late spring to early fall and can last up to 5 weeks.

This orchid is native to Madagascar, specifically found in the humid rainforests of the eastern part of the island.

The flowers emit a delightful fragrance, especially at night, which helps attract nocturnal pollinators and adds to the plant's appeal.

Angraecum magdalenae has a pseudobulb growth form, allowing it to store water and nutrients, which is beneficial during dry periods.

Lycaste Virginalis

What Makes This Orchid Special?

Common Name: "La Monja Blanca" (The White Nun), Guatemala's national flower.

Forms: Exists in various colour forms, most commonly pale pink, but also darker pinks, plum colours, and a prized albino form (f. alba).

Flower Characteristics: Spectacular, delicate, crystalline texture, long-lasting (up to 6 weeks for albas).

Fragrance: Non-fragrant, contrary to some online sources.

Size: Robust orchid with pseudobulbs 3-4" tall and leaves 18-24" long. Flowers typically 4.25-5‚Äù wide, but exceptional specimens can exceed 7-8" in diameter.

Hybridization: Known to hybridize in nature with other Lycaste species, contributing to its colour variation.

Distribution: Historically widespread from Chiapas, Mexico, through Guatemala and into Honduras. Currently rare due to deforestation and over-collection.

Conservation: Alba form listed on CITES Appendix II, complicating legal international trade. Considered rare or locally extinct in some areas.

Cultivation: Prefers cool, bright conditions, well-drained media, and regular fertilization. Sensitive to overwatering and fungal spotting.

This orchid grows as a pseudobulb-forming perennial, meaning it has swollen stems that store water and nutrients, which help it survive through dry periods.

While it can be more demanding than some other orchids, it is still a favorite among enthusiasts due to its exquisite blooms and pleasant fragrance.

After blooming, Lycaste virginalis requires a rest period where watering is reduced significantly. This period is crucial for preparing the plant for the next growing and blooming cycle.

Phalaenopsis Amabilis

What Makes This Orchid Special?

Orchid characteristics and descriptions: All sources provide detailed descriptions of the orchids' physical attributes, including leaves, pseudobulbs, inflorescence, flower colour and size, and fragrance.

Cultivation: Specific cultivation requirements for each species are discussed, encompassing light, mounting, watering, humidity, fertilization, and air circulation. Natural habitat and distribution: Information on the native range and habitat preferences of each orchid is given, including elevation, preferred trees for epiphytic growth, and geographical distribution.

Conservation: The Lycaste virginalis source focuses heavily on conservation concerns, highlighting the species' declining wild populations due to over-collection and habitat loss.

Historical significance: The Lycaste virginalis and Phalaenopsis amabilis sources delve into the historical significance of these orchids, including their introduction to European horticulture, naming controversies, and cultural associations.

Phalaenopsis amabilis is a popular parent in orchid hybridization, leading to a wide variety of cultivated hybrids that come in numerous colors and patterns.

In the wild, its flowers attract moths for pollination, which is why they resemble the shape of a moth in flight.

Phalaenopsis orchids are often recommended for beginners due to their relatively easy care requirements compared to other orchid species.
